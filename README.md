**Project Summary**  
The goal was to analyze a healthcare dataset and answer real operational questions a hospital might actually care about.  
**Dataset:** 200 patients, 15 doctors, 817 visits, 1,083 lab tests across 2023–2024  
**Domain:** Healthcare Operations  
**Type:** Portfolio Project

**Business Objective**
- Understand patient volume and visit patterns
- Monitor doctor workload and hospital capacity
- Track treatment costs, lab efficiency, and revenue performance

**Tools Used**
- PurposeSQL (SQLite) - Data extraction, transformation, KPI calculation
- Excel - Pivot tables, quick summaries, static dashboard
- Power BI - Interactive visuals, DAX measures, cross-filtering
- Looker Studio - Story-driven report with 3 sections

**KPIs Analyzed**

**Patient & Visit Metrics**
- Total Patients - 200
- Total Visits - 817
- Average Patient Age - 48 years
- Follow-Up Rate - 38% (311 visits)

**Clinical & Diagnostic Metrics**
- Top Diagnosis - Diabetes Type 2 (37 patients)
- Total Lab Tests - 1,083
- Abnormal Lab Results - 28% (307 tests)
- Highest Abnormal Rate - Depression / Asthma / Anemia (34%)

**Doctor & Revenue Metrics**
- Total Doctors - 15
- Avg Patients per Doctor - 54
- Busiest Doctor - Dr. Harish Iyer (67 patients)
- Avg Treatment Cost - ₹4,393
- Total Revenue - ₹38,77,545
- Top Revenue Department - General Medicine (₹5,87,809)

**Key Insights**
1. Lifestyle diseases dominate — Diabetes Type 2 and Hypertension make up 35% of all patients
2. High abnormal lab rate — 28% of tests flagging issues; Depression, Asthma & Anemia worst at 34%
3. Uneven doctor workload — 60% gap between busiest (67) and least busy (42) doctor
4. Psychiatry underperforming — generating less than half the revenue of other departments
5. Visit volume is growing — clear upward trend from Jan 2023 to Dec 2024
6. Mumbai leads, Delhi underrepresented — geographic imbalance in patient distribution

**Recommendations**
1. Launch a lifestyle disease prevention & screening program
2. Increase follow-up monitoring for Depression, Asthma & Anemia patients
3. Rebalance doctor workload across departments
4. Investigate and improve Psychiatry department performance
5. Plan for capacity as visit volume continues to grow
6. Focus outreach on underrepresented cities like Delhi

**Note on Dataset**  
The dataset used in this project was synthetically generated to simulate a real-world hospital environment. This is standard practice for portfolio projects involving sensitive domains like healthcare.
